Super Resolution Frequency


background


When considering the main challenges for super resolution task, there are mainly three issues existing. The first issue is about the similarity, which means whether the generated high resolution image is similar to the ground-truth. The second issue is about the efficiency, which means either training SISR the model or applying inference using the model only requires limited time or data. The third issue is about robustness, which means whether can use specific dataset to generate model adaptive to unseen data with different features.


When considering the frequency value of the whole image, it is found that normally the low frequency area is larger than the high frequency area for the image with simple structure, just as the edge part is usually less amount than the surface part. However, for images with complex structure, the ratio of high frequency region and low frequency region is indeterminate.


Single image super resolution (SISR) task is to transform the low resolution image into the corresponding high resolution image, and every detail is taken into consideration by the model. SISR is usually realized by interpolation relevant methods before the existence of deep learning techniques. Interpolation methods, such as bicubic interpolation, are feasible to provide promising solutions for low frequency region. Following is one simple experiment to test how bicubic interpolation performs on different frequency regions.


Firstly, select one random image pair with the same content. Then, apply the interpolation method on the low resolution image to generate high resolution image. The research instance above is attempting to use frequency information to further improve the similarity performance (PSNR). However, it cannot enhance the state-of-the-solution any further. One promising research direction is using frequency information to improve the efficiency, and the motivation is that for the low frequency region, the classical method such as bicubic interpolation can perform well, therefore, if costly method is only applied on the high frequency region precisely, the overall solution would become lightweight.

HFFB (high frequency focused block) is the basic component of the HBBN (high frequency focused network). The main mechanism has 2 objectives. The first objective is to extract the high frequency region and the second objective is to reduce the redundance of low frequency region.


From the research instances mentioned above, it can be learnt that “high frequency focusing methods” could be potentially beneficial for the SISR on the aspect of three issues - performance, efficiency and robustness.
